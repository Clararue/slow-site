theory | personal | neurology

Theoretical background

Speed has become our dominant ideology. The faster the data, the more valuable the interaction. Yet attention, as a human capacity, cannot be accelerated indefinitely.

Byung-Chul Han writes of the "burnout society" — a collective exhaustion produced by endless self-optimization. This site refuses efficiency, embracing latency as resistance.

Personal reflections

I made this space as an act of defiance. A place without metrics, without performance. Here, slowness feels like a small act of care.

Each delay is intentional — each pause a moment to breathe.

Neurological background

Our nervous systems are not optimized for constant input. Attention is rhythmic, not linear. It needs gaps — moments of rest — to integrate experience.

Slowness online can be seen as a neurological necessity rather than a nostalgic gesture.
